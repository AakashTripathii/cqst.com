Mahatma Gandhi

Biography

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, was a leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule.

Key Highlights of His Life:

  • Early Life and Education: Gandhi studied law in London and later moved to South Africa, where he began his activism against racial discrimination1.

  • Return to India: In 1915, he returned to India and became a leader in the Indian National Congress, advocating for various social reforms and independence

    • Major Movements

    • Satyagraha (1917)
    • Non-Cooperation Movement (1920)
    • Salt March (1930)
    • Quit India Movement (1942)

    Legacy

    Gandhi is remembered for his philosophy of non-violence and his role in India's struggle for independence.
    He is often referred to as the “Father of the Nation” in India and continues to be a global symbol of peaceful resistanc
